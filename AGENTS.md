# HDN Website Editing Rules

This project is the HDN corporate website prototype. Follow these rules whenever editing the site.

## Positioning

- Write the public site from HDN's point of view.
- Do not make ROOTS the subject of public-facing copy.
- Do not mention internal production logic such as "anonymous sample cases", "temporary structure", "for the first version", or "hard to publish real names".
- HDN's core value is turning existing patient relationships into patient journeys that lead to consultation, repeat visits, self-pay care, products, referrals, and long-term communication.
- LHub is a key asset, but the first-view message should stay broader than LINE. Use language such as patient communication, LTV, patient CRM, and patient journey.
- The site may mention that Chappy and Codex can be used to continuously improve the website, but frame it as a practical update workflow: Chappy organizes messaging and Codex implements HTML/CSS, images, and page edits.

## Audience

The site should speak to:

- Insurance-based clinics in urban and rural areas.
- Clinics doing beauty or self-pay medicine but struggling with acquisition, repeat visits, or follow-up.
- Companies and investment firms considering healthcare entry or clinic M&A.
- Dental clinics considering medical-dental collaboration.
- Clinics that know acquisition is a problem but do not know where to start.

## Voice

- Keep copy practical, calm, and businesslike.
- Avoid overclaiming outcomes.
- Avoid expressions that sound like an internal memo.
- Prefer "what HDN does for the clinic" over abstract marketing phrases.
- Keep Japanese natural and client-facing.

## Brand And Visuals

- HDN corporate color is fresh red.
- LHub service colors are navy and green.
- Use the provided HDN logo in `assets/hdn-logo.png`.
- Use `assets/hadano-profile.jpg` for the representative profile.
- Use lightweight JPG images for site visuals.
- Do not add large unused PNG source images to the deliverable zip.

## Pages

- `index.html`: main corporate page for HDN.
- `self-pay.html`: dedicated self-pay care implementation support page.
- `lhub.html`: dedicated LHub page. Use navy and green, and explain features selectively for medical institutions.
- `consultation.html`: free consultation and patient journey diagnosis page.

## CTA

- Primary CTA should lead to free consultation or patient journey diagnosis.
- Avoid making the first CTA too narrow, such as only "LINE consultation".

## Website Operations

- Present the website as something HDN can keep improving after launch.
- Mention Chappy and Codex only when it helps explain fast, flexible website updates.
- Avoid sounding like an internal tool demo. The client-facing value is continuous improvement of copy, structure, images, CTA, and content based on patient response and service changes.

## Pricing

- Keep the 3-plan structure: LIGHT, STANDARD, FULL.
- Make differences between plans clear and practical.
- Avoid dumping every item from sales decks. Reconstruct only what the target personas need to compare plans.

## Verification

- After edits, search for internal-facing terms and remove them from public copy.
- Check for broken image references.
- Create a new versioned zip after meaningful changes.
